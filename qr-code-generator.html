Introduction to the QR Code Generator

In modern marketing, logistics, and web design, QR (Quick Response) codes are a key tool for connecting physical and digital spaces. A QR code is a 2D barcode that stores data in a grid of black squares on a white background, which can be scanned by smartphones. The free **QR Code Generator** by Enginewheels helps you create custom high-resolution codes instantly.

Many online generators process your links or credentials on their backend servers, which presents a security risk if your codes contain Wi-Fi passwords, contact details, or private links. Our tool operates entirely client-side. The QR code is generated in your browser and drawn on an HTML5 canvas, so no data is uploaded to our servers, keeping your information private.

How To Use the QR Code Generator

Generating custom QR codes with our tool is quick and simple. Follow these steps to create your codes:

  1. Enter Value: Paste your link, phone number, email address, or plain text into the input field.
  2. Set Dimensions: Adjust the size slider to scale the QR code. Larger sizes (like 300px or 400px) are best for print materials.
  3. Select Error Correction: Choose an error correction level. Higher levels (like Quartile or High) allow the code to be scanned even if part of the pattern is covered or damaged.
  4. Customize Colors: Use the color pickers to customize the pattern and background colors. Ensure there is enough contrast for scanning.
  5. Save/Download: Click "Update QR Code" to preview, then "Download PNG" to save the high-resolution image to your device.

Benefits of Custom QR Codes

QR codes help simplify how users access links and information. Instead of typing long URLs, users can scan a code to open a link instantly, which is ideal for posters, business cards, menu displays, and product packaging.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can QR codes generated here expire?
No. The QR codes generated on this website are static, meaning they encode the raw text or link directly. They do not routing through middle-man servers, so they will work indefinitely.
What is the best format for printing generated QR codes?
Our download button saves the QR code as a high-resolution PNG image, which is ideal for websites, emails, or small print. For large banner prints, select larger sizes (such as 400 or 500 pixels) before generating.
How does the error correction level affect the QR code?
Error correction (measured from L to H) allows the QR code to be scanned even if part of it is covered, dirty, or damaged. Higher levels (like Q or H) allow up to 25% or 30% data recovery, but make the code pattern more complex.
Can I customize the colors of the QR code?
Yes. Our panel includes custom color pickers for both the pattern (foreground) and background. Ensure there is sufficient contrast (e.g., a dark pattern on a light background) to keep the code scan-friendly.
Is my Wi-Fi password or private URL safe?
Absolutely. All processing is executed locally in your browser using client-side JavaScript. Your text and parameters are never uploaded to our servers.
