Introduction to the Meta Tag Generator Tool

Appearing on the first page of organic search results is a key goal for website owners. Search crawlers parse metadata in the page headers (``) to understand the content and index the page accurately. The free **Meta Tag Generator** by Enginewheels helps you compile and preview tags instantly.

Many online generator tools require transmitting your drafts, URLs, and private details to their servers for processing, presenting a security risk. Our tool operates entirely client-side. The meta tags are compiled in your browser using JavaScript, keeping your metadata configurations private.

How To Use the Meta Tag Generator

Generating meta tags with our tool is quick and simple. Follow these steps to configure your header code:

  1. Enter Base Metadata: Fill in the site title, description, and keywords. Keep descriptions under 160 characters to prevent them from being cut off in search results.
  2. Configure Crawling: Select index and follow rules for search robots, and define author details.
  3. Activate OpenGraph & Twitter Cards: Check the toggles and enter a preview image link to configure social sharing cards.
  4. Generate & Copy: Click "Generate Tags" to compile, review the visual mockups, and click "Copy Code" to save the HTML snippet to your clipboard.

Metadata tags define how your website appears on search results pages and social media platforms. Well-written titles and engaging description cards help attract users and increase click-through rates from search results.

Our Meta Tag Generator automates the compilation of this code, ensuring your tags follow standard structures. The responsive layout scales smoothly, allowing you to generate and preview tags on any device.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where should I place the generated meta tags?
The generated meta tags should be copied and pasted directly into the head element (between <head> and </head>) of your HTML document.
What are OpenGraph tags used for?
OpenGraph tags are structured metadata developed by Facebook. They control how your website link is represented when shared on social platforms like Facebook, LinkedIn, or Slack, defining title, description, and image cards.
Does this tool support Twitter Card tags?
Yes. Our tool generates standard Twitter card tags, supporting both 'summary' and 'summary_large_image' card styles.
Is my input data uploaded to a server?
No. The Meta Tag Generator operates entirely locally in your browser using client-side JavaScript. Your text and parameters are never uploaded to our servers.
What is the recommended size for OpenGraph preview images?
We recommend an image size of 1200 x 630 pixels (an aspect ratio of 1.91:1) to ensure high-resolution display across social networks.
